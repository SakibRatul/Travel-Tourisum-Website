Ratargul Swamp Forest

Ratargul Swamp Forest


Ratargul Swamp Forest is the largest swamp forest in the country. This is a wonderful place in Sylhet to wander around and take a break from the tiring hustle and bustle. Many call Ratargul as the Amazon of Bengal. The forest is located in Goainghat upazila, 26 km from Sylhet city. To get closer to nature you have to take a canoe ride in the forest. You'll feel like a wonderful floating life on a rainy day while taking a night cruise in a dinghy! This forest is full of animal diversity and there are many species of birds. Among them, masranga, different species of bucks, doves, finches, mallards, pankauri etc. are notable. Wildlife includes- monkeys, wildebeests, squirrels, tigers etc. Besides, this forest is home to various species of snakes and snakes. Various species of birds and monkeys roam here. In some places inland the forest is so deep that sunlight cannot penetrate the leaves and reach the water.

When will Ratagul go


Travellers come here more or less all year round due to the beauty. However, the real beauty of Ratargul can be seen during the rainy season. Then half of the trees are submerged in water creating a charming look. So in short, May to early November is the perfect time for night travel.

How to go to Ratargul


If you want to go to Ratargul from any part of the country you have to come to Sylhet first. Sylhet buses leave at regular intervals from Syedabad, Mohakhali and Fakirapool bus stands in Dhaka. All major companies of the country including Unique, Shyamoli, Green Line, Saudia, Ena Paribahan and London Express have buses on the Sylhet route. Non AC bus fare on this route is Tk 450 to Tk 480. AC bus fare is 900 to 1200 taka. It takes 5 and a half to 7 hours to reach Sylhet from Dhaka by bus.

Sylhet to Ratargul


To visit Ratargul Swamp Forest first you need to go to Ambarkhana Point in Sylhet city. There is a CNG station at the airport road. From this station local CNG goes to Ratargul Bazar. The fare is 60-80 taka per person. If you want you can take full CNG reserve. In that case, the rent may be a little more than what is calculated per head of 5 people. It will take a little more than an hour. If it comes to local CNG, it will be brought down in the night market. From there you have to go to Ratargul boat ghat. You can reserve a battery operated autorickshaw from the market to the ghat. The rent will take a maximum of 50 rupees. Besides, if you take CNG, Laguna and microbus reserve from Sylhet, it goes directly to the ghat. There are three boat docks. Motor Ghat, Middle Ghat and Chowrangi Ghat. Chowrangi Ghat is the last of the three ghats. Boat hire from here is relatively cheap. 5 people can board together in one boat. Boat fare 600 to 750 taka. The boat will take about an hour and a half to go around the reservoir.

Cautions and Tips


Rainy season is the best time for overnight stay. There are some things to be careful about while traveling as the forest is full of water around then. When the forest is flooded during the monsoon, most snakes take shelter in tree branches or dry parts. So be careful around. Apart from this, there is also a nuisance of leeches at this time. If you do not know how to swim, it is important to keep a life jacket with you. Apart from this, it is better to keep an umbrella with you. Always keep one thing in mind while traveling anywhere, under no circumstances should you misbehave with the locals. Travel anywhere in the world – be courteous to the locals.
