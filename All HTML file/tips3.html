Camping Tips

Required advice


Preparing for camping is a little different than traditional travel with essential items and what to pack. Apart from the things you need, you also need to take some things for camping. When traveling somewhere, I fill many things that are very necessary or less necessary in the bag. There is almost no need to carry heavy bags yourself as there is a direct vehicle access to the destination. But if the camping site is in a remote area, keep in mind that you will be walking for miles with a heavy bag on your shoulder and carry all the essentials. Or carry items that have multiple uses for one item. In today's edition of the travel guide, let's find out about some of the must-have items when camping.

Tents:

Tents are one of the accessories for camping. There are various types of tents available in the market. Choose a tent that is waterproof, durable, lightweight and easy to carry. And whether to buy a small or a big tent depends on your needs and preferences.

Lights:

You can take optional rechargeable lights to light up the inside of your tent. A variety of smaller but brighter lights are available in the market these days.

Bagpack:

According to your height and carrying capacity, you can choose a backpack of the desired size and weight capacity to facilitate the carrying of your essentials.

Cloth:

Carry at least two sets of extra clothes for camping. Wrap the clothes in polythene if there is a risk of the clothes getting wet due to sudden rain or any other reason. Polythene/Polypack: Carry poly-bags for carrying wet clothes, non-perishable waste etc. while camping.

Food:

If you want to eat bar-b-que or cook at night, pack everything you need for cooking in a bag. And definitely keep some dry food with you. It's best to stick to foods that give you quick energy (chocolate, peanut bars). Chira-gur, instant coffee packets are also very useful in camping.

water bottle:

You can take water bottles depending on the transport and your needs. At least two refillable water bottles on either side of the bag won't wear off at all. And when the water runs out, refill it as soon as you get a chance.

Shoes:

Choose shoes with good grip. If you want, you can also carry light shoes like slippers for walking comfortably in the camp. Pearson or Pegasus rubber shoes work well for grip. Dress Selection: Wear light and comfortable clothes. In this case, loose pants, t-shirt or fatwa is the best combination. And if you feel comfortable wearing any other clothes, that's it too

First Aid Kit:

First aid kit is very important and useful item. But this thing does not need to be kept separate for everyone. Just take it collectively but be sure to check if everything you need is there.

hammock:

There is no comparison to a hammock for relaxing and swinging meals anywhere you like. Bring a light, easy-to-fold hammock. If you go camping, you can keep towels, personal medicine, wall plaster box, anti-mosquito cream, food saline, toothpaste, brush, tissue paper, Swiss army knife, stick, rope etc.

In choosing the things you need to take with you, consider the weight of the bag
