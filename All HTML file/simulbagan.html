Shimul Bagan of Sunamganj

Shimul Bagan of Sunamganj


In the spring, crimson flowers covered the surroundings. A group of birds is roaming around in the branches. The eyes of nature lovers are filled with this scene. Many people will not believe that there is such a place in Bangladesh. But yes, one such place is Shimul Bagan of Sunamganj. Spread over a hundred acres, this garden has now become a place of interest for nature lovers and travelers. Manigaon is the name of this village in North Barabadal Union of Tahirpur Upazila of the district. Next to the village is Shimul Bagan, the largest in the country, on the banks of the mighty Jadukata River. Three thousand Shimul saplings were planted in this Shimul Bagan about 14 years ago by the local chairman tree lover Zainal Abedin. He left the illusion of the world long ago. But this unique feat of his remains to this day.

Shimul Bagan of Sunamganj


March 28, 2020

According to the local residents, at the age of 12, Zainal Abedin along with his family came from Bhairab of Brahmanbaria district to Sohala village of Badaghat union, a separate township of Sunamganj district. This energetic man dropped out of school at an early age in life. After changing various professions, he once started fishing business. Tanguare Haor took the lease through his business, Inland Fisheries. He was also elected as the chairman of Badaghat Union Parishad. He thinks of doing something for the welfare of people. Love for plants was there from the beginning. He took the initiative to build this garden from his love and to deal with local problems. When the monsoon comes in the Haor area, the bank keeps breaking. Houses are swept away. And hard root plants are the first hope to solve this. From those thoughts, he planted 90 thousand karach trees in Tanguare Haor area from 1988 to 2000. He also planted several trees. He collected their seedlings locally. He used to water the trees himself during the dry season. He never gave less importance to their care. After applying Hijal Karach, he thought of doing something else. So created Shimul Bagan. Around 2002, Shimul started working on Bagan. First, holes were made at certain distances and natural fertilizers were applied there. This is how he prepared the soil. Then he planted 3 thousand shimul seedlings there. This is how the country's biggest Shimul Bagan was created. After about an age, the trees have become very large. After the death of Zainal Abedin, his son Rakhab Uddin, former chairman of Badaghat Union Parishad, took care of this garden.



Muhuri Project

Sunamganj can be directly reached by road from Dhaka. Non-AC buses of Ena Paribahan go to Sunamganj from Shyamoli Paribahan and Mohakhali bus station from Fakirapool and Syedabad in Dhaka. Rent is 500 to 500 taka. From Sunamganj new bridge, you can go directly by motor bike through Binnakuli Bazar. Although two people travel on each bike, for safety reasons only one person should ride each bike. Rent 200 taka. In addition, take an auto rickshaw from Sunamganj to Lauder Gard Bazar and from there cross Jadukata river to Shimul Bagan. The rent of an auto rickshaw suitable for five people is 600 to 700 taka.
